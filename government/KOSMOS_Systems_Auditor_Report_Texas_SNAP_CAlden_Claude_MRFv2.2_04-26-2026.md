## Executive Summary

This audit applies the KOSMOS Framework (7ES/FDP/DQD/OCF) to Texas's Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) implementation. The analysis reveals a system exhibiting **dominant extractive patterns** that systematically fail the majority of food-insecure Texans while maintaining the appearance of assistance.

### Critical Findings

**Eligibility-Need Gap:** 5.4 million Texans experience food insecurity; only 3.47 million (64%) receive SNAP benefits. Nearly **2 million food-insecure Texans** are structurally excluded from the program designed to serve them.

**Dominant Extractive Pattern Confirmed:** The system imposes procedural costs on vulnerable populations while concentrating benefits among those with sufficient privilege (stable housing, transportation, digital access, English proficiency, flexible work schedules) to navigate designed barriers. This is **privilege calibration**, not incidental exclusion.

**Administrative Collapse:** 138,000 applications backlogged (some since August 2023), 38-day average processing time (vs. 30-day federal requirement), 32% of applications experiencing delays. Federal government issued multiple warning letters threatening $8.4 million funding suspension for non-compliance.

**Recent Policy Deterioration:** The "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" (July 2025) expanded work requirements to ages 18-64, eliminated immigrant eligibility, shifted administrative costs to states, and restricted waivers. Estimated 300,000+ Texans will lose benefits.

### Dominant Pattern Priority Rule Application

Per MRF v2.2 Section 1.4: This system exhibits a dominant extractive pattern meeting both criteria:

1. **Majority burden concentration:** System excludes 36% of food-insecure population through income limits, work requirements, and administrative barriers that systematically disadvantage populations lacking transportation, stable housing, digital access, English proficiency, and flexible schedules.

2. **Structural impossibility:** System architecture cannot achieve stated purpose (food security for all eligible) even under optimal conditions, as demonstrated by persistent 2 million person eligibility-need gap, chronic processing backlogs, and federally-mandated restrictions.

**Therefore:** All FDP scores reflect the extractive baseline experience of excluded populations with **zero credit** for selective positive outcomes to privileged subpopulations who successfully navigate barriers.

### Element 3: Processing

**Documented Processing Mechanisms:**
- **Eligibility Determination:** Income verification (165% FPL gross limit), asset testing (vehicle values), household composition rules
- **Work Requirement Enforcement:** ABAWD rules (80 hours/month work/training/volunteer for ages 18-64), employment verification, 3-month time limit tracking
- **Document Verification:** Manual review of pay stubs, rent receipts, utility bills, identification, citizenship documentation
- **Interview Requirement:** Scheduled phone/in-person interview within 30 days, with English-language default
- **Benefit Calculation:** Net income calculation (20+ deductions), household size formula, Thrifty Food Plan basis
- **Recertification Cycles:** 6-month renewals (standard), 36-month renewals (elderly/disabled via TSAP)

**Processing Bottlenecks (Documented):**
- **Staff Shortage:** "Eligibility worker shortage" cited in official communications, net gain of 955 workers (April 2022-March 2023) insufficient for caseload
- **Software Dysfunction:** "Older eligibility software experiences outages," "daily glitches require manual re-entry," system failures delay statewide deposits
- **Combined Application Processing:** SNAP+Medicaid applications require cross-trained workers, creating processing logjams
- **Training Lag:** "Two months or more to train a worker to process Medicaid applications"
- **Federal Mandate Conflicts:** 30-day federal processing requirement vs. 38-day actual average creates permanent non-compliance

**Hidden Processing Characteristics:**
- **Privilege Calibration:** Procedural barriers (document requirements, interview scheduling, English-language forms, online portals) designed to pass applicants with organizational capacity, stable housing, digital access, flexible work schedules while excluding applicants lacking these advantages
- **Adversarial Design:** Processing assumes fraud unless proven otherwise (multiple verification layers, ongoing monitoring, work requirement policing)
- **Temporal Punishment:** Application delays (38 days average, some 24+ months) impose hunger periods as deterrent to future applications
- **Workfare Coercion:** Employment & Training program assignments leverage food insecurity to supply labor to employers without wage protections

**Processing Transformation Ratio:**
- **Input to Output Efficiency:** 200,000+ applications/renewals monthly → 3.47M recipients = ~58,000 new approvals/month estimate (assuming 6-month recerts)
- **Need to Service Ratio:** 5.4M food insecure → 3.47M served = **64% service rate, 36% structural exclusion rate**
- **Timeliness Compliance Rate:** 61% of applications on-time → **39% delayed beyond federal requirement**

**7ES Processing Subsystems Identified:**
1. **Income Verification Subsystem** (Pay stub review, employer contact, SSA data matching)
2. **Work Requirement Enforcement Subsystem** (Hour tracking, employer verification, penalty application)
3. **Document Management Subsystem** (Upload portals, mail processing, fax scanning, manual filing)
4. **Interview Subsystem** (Appointment scheduling, phone/in-person sessions, interpreter services)
5. **Benefit Calculation Subsystem** (Deduction application, household size adjustment, benefit level determination)
6. **Recertification Subsystem** (Renewal notices, document re-submission, re-interview scheduling)

**Processing Element Weakness:** Structurally adversarial. Processing optimized for **exclusion precision** (filtering based on privilege markers) rather than **need responsiveness** (serving food-insecure populations). Software dysfunction and staff shortages reveal capacity deliberately constrained below need level.

5. **Interview as Privilege Gate:** Scheduled appointments require transportation, childcare, work flexibility, phone access
6. **Recertification as Attrition Tool:** 6-month renewals with full re-verification create procedural exit points

**Control Centralization vs. Distribution:**
- **Highly Centralized:** Single state agency (HHSC) controls eligibility, single federal agency (USDA) controls rules, applicants have zero decision-making power

**Control Brittle Points:**
- **Single Point of Failure:** HHSC eligibility system dysfunction stalls entire state processing
- **Federal Dependency:** USDA rule changes (OBBBA) instantly alter state eligibility without state input
- **Political Vulnerability:** Administrative funding dependent on legislative appropriations, creating intentional capacity constraints
- **Software Obsolescence:** "Older eligibility software" indicates deferred maintenance, technical debt accumulation

**7ES Control Subsystems Identified:**
1. **Federal Regulatory Control** (USDA rules, congressional legislation)
2. **State Administrative Control** (HHSC policies, handbook updates)
3. **Eligibility Rule Control** (Income limits, asset tests, household composition rules)
4. **Work Requirement Control** (Hour tracking, exemption verification, time limit enforcement)
5. **Quality Control Monitoring** (Error rate tracking, payment accuracy audits, federal compliance reviews)

**Control Element Weakness:** Extractive by design. Controls function as **exclusion filters** rather than protection mechanisms. Centralized control prevents adaptation to local need variation. Recent policy changes (OBBBA) tighten controls to increase exclusion.

### FDP 1: Symbiotic Purpose (SP) - Weight 3

**Definition:** System outputs must create mutual benefit for all participants, not extract value for a privileged few.

**Natural Benchmark:** Bee pollination (flourishing for both insects and plants)

**Audit Question:** Does Texas SNAP create mutual benefit for all stakeholders (applicants, state, retailers, farmers) or extract value for selective benefit?

**Evidence:**

**Intended Beneficiaries (Food-Insecure Texans):**
- **Benefit to Served (3.47M):** Average $332/month/household, $177/person - enables grocery purchases, reduces hunger
- **Harm to Excluded (2M):** Structural exclusion from benefits maintains hunger, malnutrition, developmental harm to children, emergency health crises
- **Net Benefit Assessment:** 64% served / 36% excluded = **majority harmed by exclusion architecture**

**Retailers/Agricultural Sector:**
- **Benefit:** $356M/month Texas economic injection, guaranteed revenue stream, USDA reimbursement
- **No Corresponding Burden:** Retailers bear no costs for SNAP authorization, receive payment certainty unavailable in cash transactions

**State Government:**
- **Benefit:** Federal funding covers 100% of benefits (declining to 75-85% by 2028), political credit for "helping" while constraining access
- **Burden:** Administrative costs (50% federal match declining to 25%), but this incentivizes capacity reduction rather than expansion

**Federal Government:**
- **Benefit:** Agricultural price supports (SNAP drives food demand), political legitimacy (appearance of social safety net)
- **Burden:** Benefit costs ($4.26B/month nationally), administrative oversight costs

**Asymmetry Analysis:**
- **Retailers/Agriculture:** Pure benefit, zero burden → extractive relationship with applicants
- **State Government:** Declining federal reimbursement incentivizes exclusion → extractive relationship with applicants
- **Excluded Population:** Pure burden (hunger, malnutrition, stigma, time/opportunity costs from failed applications) → extraction victims

**Audit Parameters Trigger:**
Per MRF: "SP must be ≤3 if >10% of affected population loses access to healthcare, housing, food, or safety"
- 36% of food-insecure population excluded from food access → **EXCEEDS 10% THRESHOLD**
- 22.2% of Texas children food insecure → **EXCEEDS 10% THRESHOLD**

**Symbiotic Purpose Score:** **0.8/10**

**Rationale:** System creates extractive relationship where retailers/agriculture benefit without burden, state benefits from federal funding while constraining access through administrative barriers, and 36% of target population suffers extraction (hunger, malnutrition, opportunity costs) without benefit. Dominant pattern is extraction from vulnerable populations to subsidize agricultural sector and maintain political legitimacy of inadequate social safety net.

**Counterfactual:** Universal basic food stipend (no means testing, no work requirements, no application process) would score SP 8.5-9.2 - mutual benefit without extraction, administrative simplicity without privilege calibration.

### FDP 2: Adaptive Resilience (AR) - Weight 2

**Definition:** Capacity to self-correct when stressed, without requiring external enforcement.

**Natural Benchmark:** Forests adapting to fire cycles

**Audit Question:** When food insecurity increases or application backlogs occur, does system self-correct or require external intervention?

**Stress Events Documented:**

   - **Stress:** Food insecurity rose from 16.4% (2022) to 17.6% (2023-2024)
   - **System Response:** None. SNAP participation declined 3.47M (2024) vs. 4.04M peak (2013)
   - **External Intervention Required:** Advocacy organizations lobbying for Summer EBT, benefit increases
   - **Self-Correction Capacity:** 0/10

2. **Application Backlog Crisis (2023-2025):**
   - **Stress:** 258,000 applications backlogged (May 2023), oldest from August 2023 (24+ months)
   - **System Response:** Staff shuffling ("moving 250 staff from other projects"), not capacity expansion
   - **Outcome:** Backlog reduced to 79,356 (May 2024), then increased to 138,000 (June 2025) → **worsening trend**
   - **External Intervention Required:** Federal warning letters (3 from USDA, 1 from CMS), threatened $8.4M funding suspension
   - **Self-Correction Capacity:** 0/10

3. **Policy Tightening (OBBBA July 2025):**
   - **Stress:** Work requirements expanded (ages 18-64), immigrant eligibility eliminated, waivers restricted
   - **System Response:** None. State implementing federal mandates without local adaptation
   - **Projected Impact:** 300,000+ Texans lose benefits (national CBO estimate proportional to Texas)
   - **Self-Correction Capacity:** 0/10

**Autonomous Correction Mechanisms:**
- **None Documented.** All corrections require external intervention (federal warnings, media pressure, advocacy campaigns)

**External Intervention Dependencies:**
- Federal compliance monitoring (USDA Quality Control, payment accuracy audits)
- Federal funding threats ($8.4M suspension warning)
- Media exposure (investigative journalism on backlogs)
