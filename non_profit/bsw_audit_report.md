## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Baylor Scott & White Health operates as a **Hybrid-Unnatural system** (Global FDP: 4.8) exhibiting significant structural vulnerabilities masked by clinical excellence branding. The system demonstrates three critical failure modes:

1. **Vertical Integration Extractivism**: BSW controls the full healthcare value chain (hospitals + insurance + physicians), creating information asymmetries that systematically disadvantage patients while enriching executives.

2. **Legal Shield Exploitation**: Texas' $250,000 medical malpractice cap enables negligence externalization, evidenced by the Eddie Bernice Johnson case where severe infection-related death resulted in settlement rather than accountability.

3. **Nonprofit Extraction**: Despite $8.4B revenue and $1.567B claimed "community benefit," CEO compensation ($3.6M) exceeds 48× median worker pay while billing errors systematically overcharge vulnerable patients.

**Collapse Risk (OCF):** 0.52 (Moderate-High) - System depends on patient belief in nonprofit mission while operating extractive economic model. Vulnerability to mass defection if vertical integration opacity becomes visible.

**Primary Recommendation:** External enforcement dependency (ED: 0.73) indicates system requires regulatory intervention. Without structural reform, BSW represents an unstable equilibrium between stated healing mission and profit maximization.
